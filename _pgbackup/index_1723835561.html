Dr. Michaela Shields

Clinical Neuropsychologist

About:

Dr. Shields is a Clinical Neuropsychologist at the Utah State Hospital and Affiliate Faculty at Brigham Young University. She serves as the liaison between the Utah State Hospital and BYU for the completion of a clinical trial investigating the effects of Cognitive Remediation for an inpatient psychiatric population. Her clinical interests include geriatric neuropsychological assessment, neurodegenerative disorders, and cognitive remediation. Her other interests include hiking, skiing, and spending time with my corgi, Winston.

Education:

08/2017 – 07/2022 Doctor of Psychology, PsyD Clinical Psychology Training Program (APA-Accredited) Georgia Southern University (GSU) Dissertation: Developmental and Individual Differences in Visual Word Search Dissertation Chair: Ty W. Boyer, Ph.D.

08/2017 – 05/2020 Master of Science Clinical Psychology Training Program (APA-Accredited) Georgia Southern University (GSU)

08/2012 – 12/2016 Bachelor of Arts (Magna Cum Laude) University of North Carolina at Wilmington (UNCW) Major: Psychology; Minors: Biology, Neuroscience, Criminology Thesis: A Placebo-Control Evaluation of Neurofeedback Efficacy in Adults with ADHD Thesis Chair: Julian Keith, Ph.D..

Clinical Faculty Positon:

03/2023 – Present Utah State Hospital – Provo, Utah Staff Neuropsychologist, formerly Staff Psychologist.

The Utah State Hospital is an inpatient psychiatric hospital that serves the state of Utah. Populations served include pediatric (youth and adolescents ages 8-18), civilly committed adults and older adults, and adults remanded by the courts. The Neuropsychology Post-Doctoral Training Position adheres to the standards set forth by APPCN and is an APPCN member program.
